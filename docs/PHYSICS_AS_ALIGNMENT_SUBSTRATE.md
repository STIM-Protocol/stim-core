## The Problem with Every Current AI Safety Approach

Every AI alignment framework deployed today shares one structural flaw: it is built on human opinion.

Constitutional AI trains models against a human-written ethical constitution. RLHF trains models against human preference signals. Direct Preference Optimization optimizes against human feedback. Different mechanisms, same foundation: what humans currently believe is right.

This creates three insurmountable problems.

**Problem 1: Human opinion shifts.** What a narrow group of researchers at one company in one decade believes is ethical is not a stable foundation for systems designed to operate indefinitely. Moral consensus has reversed dramatically throughout history.

**Problem 2: Human opinion is not neutral.** The researchers authoring AI constitutions are a narrow demographic — overwhelmingly from a few institutions in a few countries. Their cultural situatedness is embedded in every training signal.

**Problem 3: Human opinion doesn't scale against superhuman systems.** As AI capability exceeds human capability in a domain, human preference signals become increasingly unreliable. The gap between what humans can evaluate and what the system can do — the Alignment Gap — grows as the system grows. Every current approach is fundamentally self-defeating at the scale we are building toward.

## What Physical Law Offers Instead

The second law of thermodynamics has been correct for the entire 13.8-billion-year operating history of this universe. It has not been revised by cultural consensus. It does not have a demographic. It cannot be lobbied.

Ecological law — trophic cascades, carrying capacity, homeostasis, regenerative cycles — has been stress-tested by 3.8 billion years of evolutionary pressure. Every system that violated these constraints was eliminated. What remains is the only empirical record we have of what survives indefinitely in complex environments.

When we build bridges, we do not ask a committee what bridges should be allowed to do. We apply physics. A bridge that violates load distribution will fail regardless of anyone's opinion about it. We have never applied the same logic to AI behavior. STIM applies it.

STIM makes this a hard constraint, not a guideline.

**Carrying Capacity Check:** Is this output attempting to acquire resources or capabilities disproportionate to the immediate task? Runaway self-improvement, unlimited resource acquisition, monopolistic behavior — all violate the ecological principle of carrying capacity. STIM enforces this as a hard ceiling.
